Project

USAVE ("Understanding Saving in Europe") is an Erasmus+ strategic partnership (Project ID: 2019-1-LI01-KA203-000125) that develops online courses and interactive web applications for life-cycle saving and investment literacy.

Optimal life-cycle saving and investment decisions require concepts that go beyond the time value of money and the trade-off between risk and return. Topics include human capital valuation, consumption smoothing over the lifetime, the role of insurance (disability, longevity), portfolio construction with multiple asset classes, and stochastic retirement planning under uncertainty. The project makes these concepts accessible through two self-paced online courses — one for the general public and one for finance/economics students — accompanied by hands-on interactive applications.

USAVE builds on the success of UNPIE and UNPIE2 (Understanding Pensions in Europe), extending the scope from pension decisions to the full life-cycle savings problem. It is a collaboration between the University of Liechtenstein, the Free University of Bozen-Bolzano, and Alguru ApS (Denmark).

Outcomes

Online Courses (MOOCs)

Two self-paced online courses on life-cycle saving and investment decisions, available free of charge:

  • • Course 1 — for the general public (no prior knowledge required): covers time value of money, human capital, basic savings plans, and the role of insurance.
  • • Course 2 — for finance/economics students: adds utility theory, consumption smoothing, portfolio optimization, and stochastic planning.

Interactive Web Applications

A suite of browser-based calculators and visualizations — powered by WebR (R compiled to WebAssembly) — enabling hands-on exploration of personal finance concepts:

  • • Human capital valuation and life-cycle consumption planning
  • • Disability insurance and its effect on savings
  • • Historical portfolio analysis (Investment Dashboard)
  • • Stochastic wealth paths and retirement ruin probability
